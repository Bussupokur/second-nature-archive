# Second Nature
## Why

---

She woke early without meaning to.

The house was doing what it did — temperature, light, the quiet background of things managed — and she moved through the morning the way you move when there's nowhere to be and no reason to hurry. Tea. The garden door open, the air still carrying the night's cool underneath the warmth that was already building. She took her cup outside and sat.

The sun was coming up over the far edge of the garden. That particular quality of early light — not yet committed to the day, still deciding. A breeze moved through, not cold, just present, and the curtains in the open doorway lifted and fell in a slow motion that was almost deliberate, almost choreographed, the kind of thing you only notice when your mind is already still.

She picked up the book she'd left on the table the evening before. Looked at it. Put it down.

Sat.

The garden did what gardens do in the early morning. Birds. The small sounds of things going about their business without reference to her. The breeze again. The curtains again.

Her mind was quiet. Not empty — quiet. The particular quality of a morning when nothing is required and nothing is pressing and the day has not yet made any demands. It was okay. It was fine to just sit here and let it be what it was.

She sat for a while longer.

Then she stood up, went inside, walked to the terminal.

**Why is the world the way it is?**

The system showed her this:

---

*It has been two months since his mother died. Eaten by a monster, cancer. Pain and more pain, physical pain and the agony of knowing you are going to die. Anxiety, fear. The unknown and the helplessness from her and the ones around her.*

*And here we are, we are so proud of our technological progress, AI, a marvel. It can do everything better, so much potential and yet we mostly fight for data, data for wars and better economics. Very little incentive for human progress. If we would put human well-being first. If we made that choice, now or longer ago. Would we have better treatments, cures already. Would my mother and many others still be alive.*

*Probably a cure for cancer would be very expensive. Businesses need to make huge profits, the economy must keep growing, expanding, but nobody knows why, the only goal is growth. I can hear people say, everything will be better but why does everything need to be better? What's wrong with good enough?*

*I hope one day technology will make things better, for everybody, not just a few wallets.*
