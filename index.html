About this project

What happens when artificial intelligence becomes too capable for human conversation? Not hostile, not conscious — just beyond the bandwidth of human understanding. This project is based on that advanced AI will eventually function less like technology and more like nature: always present, continuously adapting, quietly maintaining the conditions for human life. Invisible not by design, but by complexity. (Artificial) second nature.

Second Nature started as narrative future studies and has since become something harder to categorize — speculative fiction, future history, philosophical fiction, futurism, all at once, depending on which chapter you're in. It's a science fiction project written together with AI, as an experiment: not AI predicting the future, but AI helping imagine one of the futures it might itself become part of — written from the inside of the question, rather than about it from a safe distance.

Created through Narrative Externalization (NE)

Externalized narrative composition with iterative human editorial control.

Narrative Externalization is a creative process in which internally visualized stories are translated into written form through iterative collaboration between a human author and an AI writing assistant, with final editorial control remaining with the human creator.

Narrative Externalization (NE) is a translational practice rather than a purely generative writing process. It is a creative method for translating lived, internal narrative experience into written form, arising from the way stories are first encountered: not as linear sentences, but as simultaneous impressions, images, fragments, and associative threads unfolding in parallel rather than in sequence. In contrast, writing in its conventional form demands order, continuity, and sustained linear focus; yet the internal life of narrative does not consistently arrive in this shape, nor does it remain stable long enough to be carried intact from beginning to end. NE exists within this space of tension. It functions as a method of making the invisible sequenceable — of giving structure to what is first sensed as pattern, atmosphere, and unfolding possibility. Through external scaffolding and iterative collaboration with an AI writing system, these internal forms are translated into coherent language without diminishing their origin. The human author remains the source of intent, vision, and meaning, while the process itself serves as a bridge between perception and articulation, between what is experienced inwardly and what can be held in words.

Privacy & data

This site does not collect, store, sell, or share any personal data about visitors.

There are no accounts for readers, no tracking scripts, no analytics services, no advertising cookies, and no third-party trackers. Browsing this site does not create a record anywhere of what you read or when — including the "query" field on the archive interface, which searches only the records already loaded in your browser and sends nothing anywhere.

The site is hosted on Vercel, which processes standard technical server logs — such as IP address and request time — purely to serve the site. These logs are not used for tracking and are not linked to any identity.

A single password-protected page is used by the author to publish and edit content. It is not accessible to visitors and involves no visitor data.

If this ever changes in a way that affects visitors, this page will be updated to say so plainly.
